# Client Intake Request — Cascadia Aerospace

**From:** Dana Kowalski, Chief Structures Engineer, Cascadia Aerospace
**To:** Your engineering team
**Date:** April 14, 2026
**Subject:** Pylon Fairing Redesign — Cascadia 220

We're pursuing a supplemental type certificate for our turbofan pylon on the
Cascadia 220 regional jet. The current pylon design meets FAR 25.571 damage
tolerance requirements but we're seeing higher-than-expected buffet loads at
the aft engine mount during crosswind operations above 25 knots.

We need a structural redesign of the pylon fairing and potentially the thrust
link geometry to reduce aeroelastic coupling. The redesign must maintain
existing engine QEC (Quick Engine Change) interfaces and stay within the
current weight allocation of 340 kg.

We'd like an engineering assessment of the load path modifications and
updated FEM results before we approach our DER for conformity review.

Our flutter analysis team has identified a coupling mode between the first
pylon bending mode (12.3 Hz) and the wing torsion mode (14.1 Hz) that
narrows to a 6% margin at Mach 0.72 / FL350. Current certification basis
requires 15% margin per AC 25.629-1B.

Timeline: We need a preliminary assessment within 6 weeks to support our
STC project timeline. Full redesign package needed by Q3 2026.

Budget: $180K for engineering services through preliminary design review.
