# Client Intake Request — Helios Fusion Systems

**From:** Dr. Raj Patel, VP of Engineering, Helios Fusion Systems
**To:** Your engineering team
**Date:** April 14, 2026
**Subject:** First Wall Panel System — HFS-3 Pilot Plant

We're scaling our sheared-flow-stabilized Z-pinch reactor from our FuZE-Q
prototype to a pilot plant configuration (HFS-3). The first wall liner sees
cyclic thermal loads of approximately 10 MW/m² during each pulse cycle
(rep rate targeting 1 Hz, 50 ms pulse duration).

We need mechanical design support for a modular first wall panel system:
actively cooled tungsten-copper composite tiles on a reduced-activation
ferritic-martensitic (RAFM) steel backing structure. Key challenges:

- Thermal fatigue life at >10⁶ cycles (CuCrZr interlayer between W tiles
  and RAFM backing)
- Neutron-induced swelling of the RAFM steel over the planned 2-year
  replacement interval (estimated 5–10 dpa)
- Remote handling compatibility for panel replacement during maintenance
  campaigns (panels must be removable by robotic manipulator through a
  1.2m port)
- Coolant manifold routing: each panel requires 8 L/min helium at 400°C,
  8 MPa inlet pressure

Our plasma physics team can provide the heat flux profiles and neutron
fluence maps. We have ANSYS thermal-structural models of the current
prototype panels that we can share.

Timeline: Conceptual design review in 8 weeks. We're targeting first
plasma for HFS-3 in Q2 2028.

Budget: $250K for mechanical design services through CDR.
