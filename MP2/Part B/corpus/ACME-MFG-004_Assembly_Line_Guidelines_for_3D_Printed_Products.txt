ASSEMBLY GUIDELINES FOR 3D PRINTED PRODUCTS
Document: ACME-MFG-004 Rev B
Author: Maria Santos, Fab Team Lead
Date: December 1, 2025

These guidelines apply to all ACME products assembled from 3D printed PLA/PETG parts. Refer to this document when designing parts for production assembly.

SNAP-FIT DESIGN RULES
- Cantilever snap-fits: minimum cantilever length 8 mm for PLA
- Maximum deflection: 1.5 mm at engagement for PLA (beyond this, risk of cracking during assembly)
- Include a 45-degree lead-in chamfer on the hook to reduce assembly force
- Design for one-time assembly unless specifically designed as removable (different geometry needed)

PRESS-FIT RECOMMENDATIONS
For PLA press-fits on our Prusa MK4S printers:
- Structural connections: 0.10-0.15 mm interference
- Alignment-only fits: 0.05-0.08 mm interference
- Always test press-fits on a minimum of 5 samples before committing to production dimensions — printer calibration drift can shift fits from tight to loose

PIN DESIGN
Minimum pin diameters for printed pins:
- Structural pins (load-bearing pivots, axles): 2.5 mm minimum
- Alignment-only pins (locating features, non-load-bearing): 2.0 mm minimum
Below 2.0 mm, pins become too fragile for production handling — they snap during assembly or shipping. If you need a pin smaller than 2.0 mm, use brass pin stock from our component library instead of printing it.

PRINT ORIENTATION GUIDELINES
- Gears: flat on build plate (axis perpendicular to plate). This puts tooth bending loads across the full face width of interlayer bonds.
- Living hinges: print along layer lines so the hinge flexes with the layers, not against them.
- Snap-fits: print upright so the cantilever deflects along layers, not across them.
- Housings: orient so that critical fit surfaces (bearing bores, gear mesh pockets) are on the top face for best accuracy.

ASSEMBLY TIME
Target: under 10 minutes per unit for production assembly. Our experience with WidgetBot 2.0 (11 parts, 7 minutes average) and GripperBot kit (14 parts, 9 minutes average) shows this is achievable with good design.

RECOMMENDED TOOLS FOR ASSEMBLY LINE
- Flush cutters (support removal)
- 2 mm hex driver
- Small needle-nose pliers
- Soft rubber mallet (for press-fits)

COLOR CODING
For products with more than 8 parts, use contrasting filament colors for parts that could be assembled incorrectly. This reduces assembly errors and speeds up visual QC inspection.
