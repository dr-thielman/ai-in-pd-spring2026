COMPETITIVE TEARDOWN: HIWONDER BIGCLAW
Document: ACME-VND-002 (Internal Use Only)
Author: Kevin Park, Lead Mechanical Engineer
Date: December 10, 2025

PURPOSE
Reverse-engineer and document the Hiwonder BigClaw mechanical gripper to establish a reference baseline for the MiniClaw project.

OVERALL DIMENSIONS & CONSTRUCTION
- Overall size: 115 x 57 x 69 mm (confirmed, matches published specs)
- Weight with servo: 162 g
- Material: hard aluminum alloy, likely 6061-T6 based on color, machining characteristics, and density measurement
- Wall thickness: 0.8-1.2 mm throughout. This is only possible because aluminum has much higher strength-to-thickness than PLA. Our PLA designs must use at least 1.5 mm walls, probably 2.0 mm in load-bearing areas.
- Surface finish: anodized black, very clean CNC machining

GEAR TRAIN
The BigClaw uses a single-stage spur gear reduction:
- Pinion: 12 teeth
- Gear: 54 teeth
- Gear ratio: 54/12 = 4.5:1 (this ratio is not published by Hiwonder — we determined it by counting teeth)
- Module: approximately 1.5 (measured from tooth dimensions)
- Face width: 3.5 mm
- Pressure angle: appears to be standard 20 degrees
- Gear mesh is well-finished, likely hobbed aluminum then deburred. Very low backlash, smooth operation.

LINKAGE
The BigClaw uses a 4-bar mechanism to convert gear rotation to jaw displacement:
- Crank arm (attached to output gear): 32 mm effective length
- Coupler link: 28 mm
- Output link (jaw arm): 45 mm
- Ground link (housing pivot spacing): 38 mm
- Jaw opening range: 0-86 mm (confirmed)

The linkage geometry provides near-linear jaw displacement versus gear rotation through the middle 60% of travel. At the extremes (near full-open and full-close), there is slight acceleration, which gives a satisfying snap at full close. The mechanical advantage varies from approximately 2.5:1 at mid-travel to 4:1 near full close.

SERVO MOUNT
Standard servo horn pattern with M3 screw holes. The servo (LDX-335MG, 20 kg-cm stall torque at 7.4V) drives the pinion directly. The servo case is secured to the housing with 4 self-tapping screws.

IMPLICATIONS FOR MINICLAW DESIGN
1. The 4.5:1 gear ratio is modest — sufficient with a servo but probably too low for comfortable hand-driven operation. For the MiniClaw with a thumb wheel input, a gear ratio of 6:1 to 8:1 would provide better mechanical advantage and a more satisfying feel.

2. Module 1.5 is larger than ideal for a scaled-down PLA version. Module 1.0 is a better fit for our target size — it gives good tooth strength in PLA while keeping the gear envelope compact.

3. The linkage proportions are well-designed and the basic 4-bar topology could be adapted to our scale. The 32 mm crank arm would scale to approximately 25 mm at our 80% target size.

4. Aluminum wall thickness (0.8 mm) will not translate to PLA. Budget for 1.5-2.0 mm walls throughout the PLA housing.
