PRODUCT SPECIFICATION: GRIPPERBOT
Document: ACME-PROD-002
Author: Lisa Chang, Product Engineering Manager
Date: March 20, 2025

PRODUCT OVERVIEW
GripperBot is an educational servo-driven gripper kit designed for STEM programs and hobby robotics. Unlike the MiniClaw (which is a promotional giveaway), GripperBot is a sold product in ACME's education line.

KEY SPECIFICATIONS
- Jaw opening range: 0-60 mm
- Clamping force: 8 N at 25 mm jaw opening
- Servo: standard 9g micro servo, 1.8 kg-cm stall torque
- Material: injection-molded ABS (not 3D printed)
- Weight: 45 g without servo
- Part count: 9 pieces plus servo and fasteners

LINKAGE DESIGN
GripperBot uses a 4-bar linkage mechanism:
- Crank arm (servo output): 25 mm
- Coupler link: 40 mm
- Output link (jaw arm): 35 mm
- Ground link (housing pivot spacing): 30 mm
The linkage was optimized for near-linear force output through the middle 70% of jaw travel. At the extremes of travel, there is some non-linearity, but customer feedback has been positive on the feel.

CLAMPING FORCE MEASUREMENT
Standard test method: close jaws on a spring-loaded force gauge positioned at 25 mm opening width. Record peak force as jaws close to contact. This is our standard method for all gripper products — use this same protocol for MiniClaw testing.

LESSONS LEARNED (RELEVANT TO MINICLAW)
1. JAW ALIGNMENT: Jaw alignment is critical. Even 0.5 mm misalignment between the left and right jaws causes uneven gripping and was our top customer complaint in GripperBot v1. Solution: alignment pins on jaw arms and tight tolerance on the housing pivot bores.

2. LINKAGE PIVOTS: Pivot points need either low-friction bushings or generous clearance. For 3D printed versions, we recommend 0.2 mm radial clearance at pivot joints rather than trying to achieve bushing-quality fits.

3. FORCE FEEL: Users prefer a linear force-displacement relationship. Avoid toggle-style linkages that snap closed — they feel cheap and can pinch fingers. A well-designed 4-bar with a near-constant mechanical advantage through mid-travel gives the best user experience.

4. JAW GRIP: Smooth jaw surfaces slip on objects. We added silicone grip pads (from our component library) to the jaw faces, which improved grip force by approximately 40% without increasing mechanism force.

The GripperBot design files and test data are available on the engineering shared drive. MiniClaw designers should review the linkage analysis in particular.
