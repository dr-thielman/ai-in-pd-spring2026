MATERIAL SELECTION REPORT
Project: Duraline Automotive Rear Suspension Bracket Redesign
Project Number: REP-2024-055
Author: Dr. Sarah Hendricks, PE, PhD
Date: October 2024

OBJECTIVE
Select a replacement material for the Duraline RA-800 rear suspension bracket, currently manufactured from SAE 1045 hot-rolled steel. The client's objectives are a 25% weight reduction while maintaining equivalent fatigue life (minimum 500,000 cycles at peak load) and staying within a per-part cost increase of no more than 30%.

CANDIDATE MATERIALS EVALUATED
1. SAE 1045 Steel (baseline): Yield 530 MPa, density 7.85 g/cm3, cost index 1.0
2. HSLA 350W Steel: Yield 350 MPa, density 7.85 g/cm3, cost index 1.05
3. 6061-T6 Aluminum: Yield 276 MPa, density 2.71 g/cm3, cost index 2.8
4. 7075-T6 Aluminum: Yield 503 MPa, density 2.81 g/cm3, cost index 4.1
5. S-2 Glass/Epoxy Composite: Tensile 1,200 MPa, density 2.0 g/cm3, cost index 8.5

ANALYSIS METHOD
Ashby-style material indices were calculated for the stiffness-limited and strength-limited design cases. FEA was performed for each candidate using optimized cross-sections that exploit each material's properties. Fatigue analysis followed the stress-life approach per SAE J1099.

RECOMMENDATION
6061-T6 aluminum is recommended. While the raw yield strength is lower than the steel baseline, the 65% density reduction enables a redesigned cross-section that achieves equivalent stiffness at 38% lower weight. The thicker aluminum sections provide better fatigue performance in the bracket geometry (predicted life: 720,000 cycles vs. the 500,000 cycle requirement). The per-part cost increase is estimated at 22%, within the client's 30% budget. The 7075-T6 option offers higher strength but the 4.1x cost multiplier exceeds the budget, and the modest additional weight savings (3%) does not justify the premium. Composite options were eliminated due to cost and manufacturing complexity for the production volume (15,000 units/year).
