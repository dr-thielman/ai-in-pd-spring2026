PROJECT SUMMARY REPORT
Project: Cascade CP-2200 Portable Speaker Thermal Analysis
Project Number: REP-2024-012
Client: Cascade Consumer Products
Project Manager: Mark Jensen, PE
Date Completed: March 2024
Budget: $95,000 | Actual: $89,200

BACKGROUND
Cascade Consumer Products retained Ridgeline to evaluate the thermal performance of their CP-2200 portable Bluetooth speaker, which had experienced field returns due to thermal shutdown during extended outdoor use in direct sunlight. The speaker uses a Class D amplifier rated at 40W RMS that dissipates approximately 8W of heat at maximum output.

ANALYSIS
Ridgeline constructed a detailed thermal model using FloTHERM, incorporating the amplifier IC, battery pack (4-cell 18650 lithium-ion), PCB, enclosure, and passive radiator. Boundary conditions simulated worst-case outdoor use: 40C ambient temperature, 1000 W/m2 solar radiation on the top surface, and natural convection only (no wind).

The analysis identified that the amplifier junction temperature reached 118C under worst-case conditions, exceeding the 110C thermal shutdown threshold. The primary heat path from the amplifier IC to the enclosure surface was constrained by a 2mm air gap between the PCB and the enclosure wall. Battery temperatures reached 52C, which is within the safe operating range but reduces cycle life.

SOLUTION
Ridgeline recommended three design modifications: (1) adding a 0.5mm-thick graphite thermal interface pad between the amplifier IC and the enclosure wall, reducing thermal resistance by 62%; (2) increasing the ventilation slot area on the bottom of the enclosure by 40%; and (3) applying a selective high-emissivity coating (emissivity 0.92) to the interior surfaces near the amplifier. With all three modifications, the predicted worst-case junction temperature dropped to 98C, providing a 12C margin below the shutdown threshold. Cascade implemented all three recommendations and eliminated the thermal shutdown returns.
