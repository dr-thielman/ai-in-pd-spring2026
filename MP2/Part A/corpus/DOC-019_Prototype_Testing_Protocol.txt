RIDGELINE ENGINEERING PARTNERS — PROTOTYPE TESTING PROTOCOL
Document Number: ENG-PT-01, Revision 5
Effective Date: May 2024
Author: Ryan Cho, PE, Prototype & Testing Group Lead

This protocol establishes minimum requirements for prototype testing performed in Ridgeline's test laboratories.

1. GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

1.1 Sample Size
All prototype tests must use a minimum of 3 units to establish statistical validity. For tests intended to demonstrate reliability or fatigue life, a minimum of 5 units is required. For tests where manufacturing variation is a key concern (e.g., seal performance, snap-fit retention force), a minimum of 10 units is recommended. Sample size justification must be documented in the test plan.

1.2 Test Plan Approval
All prototype tests require a written test plan approved by the Project Manager and at least one Senior Engineer. The test plan must define: test objectives, pass/fail criteria, sample size, test procedure, equipment to be used, data to be collected, and the format of the test report.

1.3 Equipment Calibration
All measurement equipment used in prototype testing must have current calibration certificates. Calibration intervals follow manufacturer recommendations or 12 months, whichever is shorter. The Instron 5985 universal testing machine is calibrated semi-annually by Instron's field service team.

2. TEST TYPES

2.1 Mechanical Testing
Tensile, compression, and bend testing per ASTM standards. Load cell capacity: 50 kN (Instron 5985) or 5 kN (Instron 3345 for smaller specimens). Strain measurement via extensometer or digital image correlation (DIC) system.

2.2 Environmental Testing
Thermal cycling: -40C to +85C, ramp rate 5C/min, hold 30 min at extremes. Humidity: 85C/85% RH for up to 1000 hours. Salt spray: per ASTM B117, neutral salt spray at 35C. UV exposure: per ASTM G154, cycle 4 with UVA-340 lamps.

2.3 IP Rating Verification
Ingress protection testing per IEC 60529. The Portland lab is equipped for testing up to IP67 (dust-tight, temporary immersion 1m/30min). IP68 and above requires outsourcing to NTS or Intertek.

3. DATA MANAGEMENT
All test data is stored on the firm's SharePoint server under Projects > [Project Number] > Testing. Raw data files, photographs, and videos are retained for a minimum of 7 years. Test reports must be reviewed by a Senior Engineer before release to the client.
