RIDGELINE ENGINEERING PARTNERS — EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK (Rev. 8, 2024)
CHAPTER 7: PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT AND TRAINING

7.1 Continuing Education Requirements
Ridgeline is committed to the ongoing professional development of all engineering staff. To maintain technical excellence and meet professional licensing requirements, all engineers are required to complete a minimum of 24 hours of continuing education per year. At least 12 of these hours must be in technical topics directly related to the employee's practice area. The remaining 12 hours may include professional skills development, project management training, or approved industry conference attendance.

7.2 Tuition Reimbursement
Full-time employees with at least one year of service are eligible for tuition reimbursement up to $8,500 per calendar year for approved graduate coursework. Courses must be at an accredited institution and directly relevant to the employee's role. Employees must receive a grade of B or better to qualify for reimbursement. Applications are submitted through the HR portal and require manager approval.

7.3 PE License Support
Ridgeline strongly encourages all eligible engineers to pursue Professional Engineer licensure. The firm covers the cost of the PE exam (currently $375), provides up to 40 hours of paid study time, and offers a $2,500 bonus upon successful completion. Engineers who hold a PE license are eligible for accelerated promotion consideration.

7.4 Conference Attendance
Each practice group has an annual conference budget. Engineers may attend up to two approved conferences per year with full travel coverage. Priority is given to employees presenting papers or serving on technical committees. Approved conferences include ASME IMECE, AIAA SciTech, SAE WCX, IEEE ITHERM, and regional ASCE meetings.

7.5 Internal Training
Monthly 'Lunch & Learn' sessions are held at each office, covering technical topics, software tool updates, and project case studies. Attendance counts toward the 24-hour continuing education requirement. Senior engineers are expected to present at least once per year.
